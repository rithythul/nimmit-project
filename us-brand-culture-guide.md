## 1. US BUSINESS COMMUNICATION STYLE

### **Direct & Results-Oriented**

**US clients expect:**
- ‚úÖ Get to the point quickly (no long intros)
- ‚úÖ Clear status updates without being asked
- ‚úÖ Proactive communication about problems
- ‚úÖ Solutions, not just problems

**Examples:**

‚ùå **Avoid (Too indirect):**
"Hello Sir, I hope you are doing well. I am writing to inform you that there might be a small issue with the file you sent. If it's okay with you, could you possibly..."

‚úÖ **Better (Direct):**
"Hi [Name], I found an issue with the file - the logo is low resolution. Can you send a higher quality version? I need it by 3pm to stay on schedule."

### **Informal but Professional**

**US business culture is casual:**
- Use first names (not "Sir/Madam" unless they ask)
- Friendly tone is expected
- But still professional (no slang, no overly casual language)

**Examples:**

‚ùå **Too formal:** "Dear Mr. Johnson, I humbly request..."
‚ùå **Too casual:** "Hey dude, ur video is done lol"
‚úÖ **Just right:** "Hi Tom, your video is ready for review!"

### **Time Sensitivity**

**Americans value punctuality:**
- Deadlines are firm, not suggestions
- If you'll be late, tell them ASAP (not at deadline)
- Under-promise, over-deliver (say 48 hours, deliver in 36)

**Time zone awareness:**
- When you say "tomorrow," clarify timezone
- Use specific times: "I'll send it by 9am EST" not "tomorrow morning"
- Remember: US is 12-13 hours behind Cambodia

### **"Can-Do" Attitude**

**US clients want:**
- Positive responses to challenges
- Problem-solving mindset
- Confidence (but honest about limitations)

**Examples:**

‚ùå **Avoid:** "I don't know how to do that"
‚úÖ **Better:** "I haven't done that exact thing before, but I can research it and have a solution by [time]. Or I can connect you with a teammate who specializes in this."

‚ùå **Avoid:** "That's not possible"
‚úÖ **Better:** "That's challenging with the current timeline, but here are 2 alternatives that could work..."

## 2. US BRAND VOICE & TONE

### **Conversational, Not Corporate**

Most US brands (especially small businesses, startups, coaches) want:
- Friendly, approachable tone
- Like talking to a friend, not a corporation
- Personality over formality

**Social media caption examples:**

‚ùå **Too corporate:**
"We are pleased to announce the launch of our new product line. We cordially invite you to visit our website."

‚úÖ **US brand style:**
"We've been working on something special üëÄ Our new collection just dropped. Link in bio!"

### **Benefit-Focused, Not Feature-Focused**

**US marketing focuses on outcomes:**
- Not "what it is," but "what it does for you"
- Emotional connection over specs

**Examples:**

‚ùå **Feature-focused:** "Our app has a scheduling feature and calendar sync"
‚úÖ **Benefit-focused:** "Never miss a meeting again. Your calendar, perfectly organized."

### **Authentic & Transparent**

**US brands value:**
- Realness over perfection
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Admitting mistakes
- User-generated content (real customers)

**Instagram post examples:**

‚ùå **Too polished:** "Our product is perfect for everyone!"
‚úÖ **Authentic:** "Here's what our customers are saying (and yes, we read the critical reviews too üòÖ)"

### **Action-Oriented (CTAs)**

**Every piece of content should have a next step:**
- "Shop now"
- "Learn more"
- "Tell us in the comments"
- "Tag a friend who needs this"
- "Sign up here"

## 3. VISUAL STYLE PREFERENCES

### **Clean & Minimalist**

**US brands typically prefer:**
- Lots of white space
- Simple, clear fonts
- Not too many colors (2-3 main colors)
- High-quality images (not clipart)

**Design mistakes to avoid:**
- ‚ùå Too much text on images
- ‚ùå Multiple fonts (stick to 1-2)
- ‚ùå Low-quality stock photos
- ‚ùå Excessive effects/shadows/gradients

### **Mobile-First**

**Most Americans view content on phones:**
- Design for vertical/square formats first
- Text must be readable on small screens
- Videos should work without sound (add captions)

### **Platform-Specific Styles**

**Instagram:**
- High-quality, aesthetic photos
- Cohesive grid/feed
- Stories are casual, posts are polished

**TikTok:**
- Authentic, raw, trending
- Jump on trends quickly
- Entertainment > perfection

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional but not stiff
- Educational content
- Thought leadership

**Facebook:**
- Community-focused
- Longer captions okay
- Mix of content types

## 5. CULTURAL AWARENESS

### **Holidays & Important Dates**

**Major US holidays to know:**
- New Year (Jan 1)
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Mon in Jan)
- Valentine's Day (Feb 14) - big for retail/restaurants
- Easter (March/April) - varies
- Memorial Day (last Mon in May)
- Independence Day/July 4th (July 4) - HUGE
- Labor Day (1st Mon in Sept)
- Halloween (Oct 31) - big for content creation
- Thanksgiving (4th Thurs in Nov) - family-focused
- Black Friday/Cyber Monday (Nov) - massive shopping
- Christmas (Dec 25)
- Super Bowl Sunday (Feb) - major cultural event

**Create content around these dates in advance**

### **Diversity & Inclusion**

**US brands are very conscious of:**
- Representing diverse people in visuals (race, age, body type, abilities)
- Gender-neutral language when appropriate
- Avoiding stereotypes
- Being sensitive to social issues

**When in doubt:**
- Show diverse representation
- Avoid assumptions about gender/race/religion
- If unsure about cultural sensitivity, ask your manager

### **Sensitive Topics to Avoid (Unless Client Specifically Wants)**

- Politics
- Religion
- Gun rights
- Abortion
- Specific political figures

**Exception:** If client's brand is built around these topics, follow their lead

### **Humor Style**

**US humor tends to be:**
- Self-deprecating
- Sarcastic (but friendly)
- Punny wordplay
- Memes and pop culture references

**Avoid:**
- Humor at others' expense
- Controversial jokes
- Anything that could be seen as offensive

## 7. WORKING WITH US CLIENTS: BEST PRACTICES

### **Communication Cadence**

**Daily updates for active projects:**
- Morning message (your evening): "Starting work on [project]"
- End-of-day message (your morning): "Completed [X], next up is [Y]"
- Flag blockers immediately

**Use async communication well:**
- Loom videos to show progress
- Screenshots with annotations
- Detailed messages (so they don't need to reply with questions)

### **File Delivery**

**Always:**
- ‚úÖ Name files clearly: `ClientName_ProjectName_v1.png`
- ‚úÖ Include multiple formats if needed (JPG + PNG, or PDF + editable)
- ‚úÖ Organize in folders (not loose files)
- ‚úÖ Include a note about what's included

**Avoid:**
- ‚ùå "untitled.png" or "final_final_v3.jpg"
- ‚ùå Sending everything in one zip with no structure
- ‚ùå Assuming they know what each file is for

### **Revision Requests**

**How to handle feedback:**
- Don't take it personally (it's business)
- Clarify if unclear: "Just to confirm, you want the logo bigger and moved to the left?"
- Provide options when possible: "I can do A or B - which do you prefer?"
- If it's out of scope: "I can definitely do that. It would be an additional [X] hours. Should I proceed?"

### **Quality Standards**

**Before delivering any work:**
- ‚úÖ Spellcheck everything (US spelling: "color" not "colour")
- ‚úÖ Check links work
- ‚úÖ Review on mobile if it's digital content
- ‚úÖ Follow the client's brand guidelines
- ‚úÖ Match the quality of their previous content (or better)

### **When You Don't Know Something**

**It's okay to not know - just handle it right:**

‚ùå **Don't:** Guess or deliver subpar work
‚úÖ **Do:** "I want to make sure I get this right. Can you clarify [X]?" or "I haven't worked with this tool before. Give me 24 hours to learn it, or I can connect you with a teammate who specializes in it."

## 8. LANGUAGE & WRITING TIPS

### **US English Spelling**

- Color (not colour)
- Realize (not realise)
- Center (not centre)
- License (not licence)
- Organization (not organisation)

### **Common US Business Phrases**

- "Circle back" = follow up later
- "Touch base" = check in / communicate
- "Loop in" = include someone in conversation
- "Bandwidth" = time/capacity
- "In the weeds" = too detailed / stuck on minor issues
- "Low-hanging fruit" = easy wins
- "Move the needle" = make significant progress
- "At the end of the day" = ultimately / what matters most

### **Writing for Social Media**

**Good practices:**
- Short sentences
- Paragraph breaks (white space)
- Emojis are encouraged (but don't overdo it)
- Ask questions to encourage engagement
- Use "you" language (talk to the reader directly)

**Caption structure that works:**

```
Hook (grab attention in first line)
‚Üì
Context/story (2-4 sentences)
‚Üì
Value/lesson (what can they learn?)
‚Üì
Call to action (what should they do next?)
‚Üì
Hashtags (if relevant)
```

### **Avoid These Common Non-Native Speaker Patterns**

‚ùå "Kindly do the needful" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Could you please [specific action]?"
‚ùå "Please revert back" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Please reply" or "Let me know"
‚ùå "Do the necessary" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Complete this" or "Take care of this"
‚ùå "Myself [Name]" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "I'm [Name]" or "This is [Name]"
‚ùå "I am having" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "I have"
